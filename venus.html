Discovery

Well Venus doesn't actually have moons− unles we haven't discovered them yer-so here's some other details. Venus is always brither than any star (apart from the Sun). The greatest luminosity, apparent magnitude -4.9, occurs during crescent phase when it is near Earth. Venus fades to about magnitude -3 when it is backlit by the Sun. The planet is bright enough to be seen in a mid-day clear sky, and it can be easy to see when the Sun is low on the horizon. As an inferior planet, it always lies within about 47° of the Sun.

Venus is always brither than the brightest stars outside our solar system.
